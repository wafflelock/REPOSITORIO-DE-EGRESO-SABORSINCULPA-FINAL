Gluten-Free Pizza

Preparation:

  • Dough:
  • In a large bowl, mix gluten-free flour, yeast, and salt.
  • Add olive oil and warm water, and mix until a homogeneous dough forms.
  • Knead the dough for 10 minutes on a floured surface until it is smooth and elastic.
  • Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let rise in a warm place for 1 hour, until it has doubled in size.
  • Sauce:
  • Heat a little olive oil in a pan over medium heat.
  • Sauté the onion and garlic until translucent.
  • Add crushed tomatoes, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  • Simmer for 15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  • Assembling the pizza:
  • Preheat the oven to 200°C (392°F).
  • Roll out the dough on a floured surface to form a thin circle.
  • Place the dough on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  • Spread the dough with tomato sauce.
  • Add your favorite topping ingredients.
  • Bake for 15-20 minutes, until the crust is golden and the cheese is melted.
  • Serve hot.

Ingredients:

  • For the dough:
  • 250 g gluten-free flour
  • 1 teaspoon gluten-free dry yeast
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 150 ml warm water
  • For the sauce:
  • 400 g crushed tomatoes
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 red onion, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • For the topping:
  • Ingredients of your choice (e.g., gluten-free mozzarella, pepperoni, mushrooms, bell peppers, olives)

Macronutrients per serving (approximate):

  • Calories: 500
  • Protein: 30 g
  • Carbohydrates: 60 g
  • Fats: 20 g
